Mia [CCR Analyst]: Thanks everyone. I’ll just jump in. Noel [UX Lead]: Okay, two things there. One, the interaction model: point and click, but also fully keyboard operable, so you can tab through, hit Enter, spacebar—everything works even without the mouse. Two, the dashboard summary—I’m hearing you say per report type, there’s at least one high-level card or tile. We can do that in a way that conforms to our design system and IBM’s usability standards.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: And don’t forget security posture. Access on W3 only, authenticated with IBM intranet credentials. No auto-logins. No caching of passwords. We’ve had issues when apps store credential cookies—that’s a hard no.

And we can add connections to additional WCS databases later… with the right approval from data owners, of course.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: Monday night would be good actually, that way Tuesday morning we get a fresh cut. If Friday, people forget by Monday. But um, either way weekly is fine. Just don’t make it random.

Noel [UX Lead]: That’s straightforward. We’ll place both marks per the brand spec. Also, we need to talk about screen resolution. Some CCR offices still have ancient monitors that only do 800 by 600 reliably. It’s… retro, but it’s real. We can design to that, but it constrains layout.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: Yeah, I’m one of those people sometimes when I work remote on a loaner. Please make the interface readable and usable at that resolution without horizontal scrolling. And no tiny text.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: On environment: just to put it on the record, this is for Windows machines, IBM-issued. Windows 2000 or above. It’s intranet-only, no external exposure. Availability is tied to W3. If W3 is up, we aim to be up—within reason. We usually say 95% of W3’s uptime as a target. Don’t quote me on the exact number—actually, yes, write it down. 95% of W3’s availability is the internal target for dependent apps like this.

Tom [WCS DBA]: For terminology, can someone confirm we’re sticking to the CCR terms already used in the existing WCS reports? Because we get requests to rename “Regional Program” to “Area” or “Zone,” and that creates a mess.

Noel [UX Lead]: Yes, we’ll do usability tests with CCR users, and we’ll validate labels and flows. Also, for first-time use, what’s the success metric? I recall we were aiming that nine out of ten users can generate a report without needing help on their first try.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: That sounds right. Most of us don’t want to read a manual to run a simple report. If I can find it, click once to run, and then hit export—Excel or, hm, do we still do Lotus 1-2-3—then I’m good.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: We still have some regions using Lotus 1-2-3 for legacy processes. Don’t ask me why. The Brio module should provide buttons to export to .xls and .123 file formats. It’s a requirement we’ve had to keep for a while.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: Guilty. Brio. Got it. As long as the export is literally a button, not hidden under three menus.

Noel [UX Lead]: Button-driven exports are fine. And again, keyboard equivalent—Alt or tab focus, spacebar activates. We’ll ensure 100% of actions are accessible via mouse or keyboard.

Raj [CCR Manager]: Good. Now, about training. Do we really need a full session for everyone before it goes live? I mean, if we nail usability, people can figure it out, right?

Mia [CCR Analyst]: I kind of agree, but then we always get someone in the field who misses an email and then can’t log in. Maybe a single training session that everyone’s invited to, recorded, and docs updated. Just one.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: Training is also an opportunity to remind people how to log in properly—using their intranet username and password—and to reinforce there’s no automatic login. We need to be explicit that the system won’t store credentials in cookies. That comes up every time.

Tom [WCS DBA]: On the connectivity to other WCS databases—there’s a push to eventually include the volunteer hours DB, but that’s not in scope at day one. However, we should be able to add designated sources later once approved. I don’t want surprises then.

Noel [UX Lead]: Understood. We’ll plan for later additions as designated by governance. But just to be clear, we’re not promising any new kinds of analytics, just the ability to hook in approved WCS sources later when cleared.

Raj [CCR Manager]: Yes, just expansion capability, governed. And about international—we need support for non-US data. Also, some of our colleagues in EMEA and APAC have asked if they can log in, and that’s fine as long as they’re approved CCR staff. So access should allow that, not US-only.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: International access is okay on W3, but approvals must be in place. We’ll check the access groups. Data for non-US regions, fine, as long as we treat it the same governance-wise.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: On learnability, I want the flow to be so obvious that after the first use, if I come back a week later, I still remember how to run it. If we say 90% remember the steps after one session, I think that’s the intent.

Noel [UX Lead]: That’s achievable. Clear navigation, consistent placement of actions, nothing buried. And we stick to IBM usability guidelines. We’ll measure task completion and recall during testing with CCR users.

Tom [WCS DBA]: Let me circle back on the weekly refresh. If it’s Monday night, it can finish by early Tuesday. Queries go out to the external WCS sources, bring in changes. But if W3 hiccups, availability may fluctuate. Our target is tied to W3 anyway, right?

Linda [IBM IT Security]: Right. If W3 is down, we’re down. If W3 is up, you aim for 95% of that window. That’s the internal SLA vernacular we use. Some folks confuse it with overall 95% uptime, but it’s specifically a fraction of the intranet availability.

Raj [CCR Manager]: Also, we need to show that our terminology matches “Corporate Community Grants” standards. Like, we’re not renaming things just to be cute. If a field says “Grant Recipient” in WCS, it should say that here too.

Noel [UX Lead]: Noted. CCR nomenclature, consistent capitalization, alignment with existing WCS reports. We’ll put those specifics in the style guide.

Linda [IBM IT Security]: Just to be annoying for a second: auto-login is not happening. Also, no storing sensitive info in cookies, including login names. We’ve had apps try to save convenience tokens. Not here.

Tom [WCS DBA]: What about performance? Sorry, tangent. I know we’re not defining a performance metric today, but if the queries hit bigger datasets once we add global, that weekly refresh needs to be efficient. I’ll optimize, but just saying.

Raj [CCR Manager]: It just needs to be fast enough that by Tuesday morning it’s done. And in the UI, I don’t want spinners spinning forever.

Noel [UX Lead]: We’ll keep the UI straightforward and indicate when results are ready without adding extra steps. For now, the weekly refresh happens on a set day, and the UI displays current data.

Mia [CCR Analyst]: Another small one: make all actions doable with keyboard, including opening the export dialog. I know you said 100%, but I’m repeating it because sometimes developers forget the last 10%.
