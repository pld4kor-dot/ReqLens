- Vision recap: fast, anonymous head-to-head. Periscope illusion. Ships look like actual classes (not blobs). Ocean feel.
- UI mask: circle viewport for boards. Outside of circle dimmed, metal rim? Owen says yes. Raj: caution on small screens.
- Two boards per player always shown (within circle): offense tracking grid and own defense grid. Opponent never sees either. No cross-board view.
- Lobby basics:
  - Maintain a list of players present. It is anonymous list, not names. [check empty state UI]
  - Players opt in to be shown. Toggle to set self as available. Can toggle off at any time. Self removal instant.
  - Inactive players should not be selectable. Hide them if no activity for some interval [?].
  - After 24 hours of no heartbeat, remove from list entirely. Hard drop. Cron? [Tasha to confirm]
  - One-click start: select an available opponent => system pairs them. Immediately both removed from lobby selection (not pickable). Lock until game end.
  - Edge case: two people clicking same person. Need atomic lock. SRE to add compare-and-swap on availability flag.

- Localization:
  - Language selection must happen at first access. Only English, Spanish, French. Persist choice per session.
  - All messages, including turn notices, sink notices, end-of-game reasons, must be localized.
  - French strings longer; watch space in circle. Icons where possible. [TBD: truncation strategy]

- Privacy:
  - Strict anonymity: no PII, no usernames, no avatars. Only presence and state.
  - Notifications must not include any identifiers. Just event-based language.
  - Opponent boards must never be visible. No spectate. No hints about layouts.

- Setup phase:
  - Each player places five ships on defense grid. Classes: [Owen to list assets].
  - Orientations allowed: horizontal, vertical, diagonal. Not zig-zag.
  - No overlap across ships. Reject placement if any cell is already occupied.
  - Off-grid placement should snap back or be blocked.
  - Players can rearrange until pressing Ready.
  - Both must press Ready; only then choose first shooter. Random assignment is fine.

- Turn engine:
  - At start of each turn, clearly notify both players of status: shooter vs targeted. Copy must be simple. Consider colors too. [Felix: test colorblind]
  - Maintain a status per player: offensive or defensive. Server is source of truth.
  - Shooter defines a shot by selecting a coordinate on their firing grid.
  - Server evaluates defender’s grid to determine hit or miss.
  - System notifies both parties of outcome: hit or miss. Keep copy concise.
  - Mark the result on both sides:
    - On defender’s defensive grid: mark cell hit or miss.
    - On shooter’s offensive grid: mirror the same hit/miss mark.
  - Sinking logic:
    - When hits result in all cells of a ship being struck, that ship is sunk.
    - Notify both players that the ship is down. Include ship class in the defender’s context.
    - On defender’s grid: show the sunk ship outline and type. On shooter’s grid: indicate that ship is sunk as well.
  - Turn continuation:
    - If hit: shooter immediately gets another shot. No status flip.
    - If miss: change roles. Shooter becomes targeted, defender becomes shooter. Update UI and notify at turn start.
  - Continue assigning turns until end-of-game condition met.

- End-of-game conditions:
  - Win condition: last remaining ship of target side sinks. Immediately end match.
  - Early end: either player can end a game at any time (resign). [Check words: End vs Surrender vs Give up]
  - On ending, notify both players that the game concluded. Include reason: victory by all ships sunk, or opponent ended, or you ended.
  - Post-match options: present two actions: relist self as available OR remove self from the lobby. Keep it simple.

- Audio plan:

  - Distinct sound for hit. Impact or metallic thud.
  - Distinct sound for sink. Longer, deeper audio cue.
  - Optional end-of-match bell. NOT decided yet. [Owen leaning yes; Mia neutral]

- Recap of rules for quick reference:
  - Five ships per player, placed on defensive grid. Horizontal, vertical, diagonal allowed. No overlaps, no off-grid.
  - After both press Ready, random first shooter assigned. Notify both of role at start of turn.
  - Offensive player selects target cell. Server computes hit or miss by checking defender’s grid.
  - Notify both: hit or miss. Mark on both boards accordingly.
  - When a ship is completely hit, it is sunk. Notify both. Defender sees class and sunk outline; attacker sees sunk indicator.
  - Hit grants another shot. Miss swaps offense and defense roles.
  - Keep alternating or continuing as per rules until end. End when all ships of one side are down, or when a player ends the match manually.
  - On game end, show end-of-game notice with reason. Then offer relist self as available or leave lobby entirely.

- Ops recap:
  - Always-on service target. Many concurrent matches. Snappy interactions to keep flow. Internationalization at entry. Anonymity throughout.
