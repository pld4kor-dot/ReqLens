Hey all,

Quick recap based on a couple of questions I got after the call.

- Lobby list: we keep a live roster of anonymous players who have opted in to be available. Clicking an available person is a one-click start. As soon as the match is created, both sides should be removed from the pickable list globally so no one else can grab them mid-handshake.
- Self removal: players must be able to toggle off at any time, with immediate effect. No waiting for a cycle.
- Inactivity: if someone is idle, do not show them as selectable. If they ghost for a day, go ahead and drop them from the list entirely.
- Turn status: after both finish placing their five ships, system decides who fires first and lets both know clearly who is shooter and who is on the receiving end. At the start of each turn, repeat that status.
- Turn loop: shooter taps a coordinate; system checks the defender board, returns miss or hit, marks both boards accordingly. On a hit, the shooter goes again. On a miss, swap roles.
- Game end: either when the last ship is sunk or if a player ends the match manually. When that happens, tell both players the match is over and why. Then give them a choice to re-enter the lobby as available, or leave.

Let me know if I missed something from your angles.

On pairing: I will implement a short-lived lock with a CAS on the availability token. That ensures once one request flips a player to in-match, others get rejected cleanly.

SLOs: For the action loop, we should set expectations. I propose we instrument so that 95 percent of turn actions round-trip under 2 seconds. And we keep the worst cases under 4 seconds end-to-end. If we are constantly above that, the duel will feel laggy.

Availability: The game has to be running 24x7. I will set up rolling deploys and redundancy so we can operate globally. No planned downtime if we can help it.

Scale: Assume lots of simultaneous matches. I am sizing for many concurrent games from the start and we will pressure test. We will design for horizontal scale.

Lobby idle handling: I will hide idle users from selection and drop users who have been inactive for 24 hours automatically. Define idle precisely in a separate doc.

— Tasha

A few constraints from compliance:

- Anonymity is paramount. No user-entered names or profiles. The lobby is anonymous presence only. Do not show any info that could be tied back to a person. Avoid even cute identifiers that could cross-expose identities.
- In the match, never reveal opponent grids. The only data the shooter sees is their own firing history. The defender sees impacts on their own grid. Do not leak any hints or patterns. No spectate mode either.
- End-of-match notices must not include identifying info. Just the reason, e.g., all ships down or opponent ended.

If you stick to these, we should be good for strong identity protection. I will review strings for any accidental leaks.

— Luis

Thanks everyone. Consolidating and confirming a couple items, and one pushback.

- Diagonal placement is allowed and must be straight, not wiggly. Five ships per player, no overlaps, and out-of-bounds blocked. Players can move ships until they hit Ready. Once both hit Ready, we randomize first shot and notify both of their roles.
- Turn start: both players get a clear status message at the start of each turn. Shooter chooses a cell on their firing grid; server checks defender grid; result comes back as hit or miss. We mark both boards. If a ship goes down due to that hit, the system declares it sunk and we say which class on the defender’s side. Mark sunk on both boards.
- Streaks: a hit grants another shot for the shooter. A miss flips roles: attacker to defender, defender to attacker. Keep that loop until the end condition.
- Ending a match: we will end when the last enemy ship is sunk, or if someone ends the game early. The end notice includes the reason. After that, present two choices: list me again in the lobby or leave the lobby. Once either option is chosen, unlock them from the previous match.
- Lobby behavior: anonymous list of people who opted to be available. Select an available person to start; both vanish from the list for others until the match finishes. Players can delist themselves any time. Hide idlers and auto-remove after 24 hours.
- UX and audio: periscope circular mask for the boards. Visual ships that look like their classes. Ambient ocean during the shooter’s turn, distinct sounds for miss, hit, and sink.
- Ops: Tasha will run this 24 by 7 and aim for most actions under two seconds, with the tail not exceeding four. Also plan for lots of concurrent games.

Pushback: I do not want to add any user identity even in a clever way. So no fun nicknames or randomized adjectives. Keep it strictly anonymous.

If anyone disagrees with any of the above, reply here. Otherwise assume this as a working baseline.

— M
