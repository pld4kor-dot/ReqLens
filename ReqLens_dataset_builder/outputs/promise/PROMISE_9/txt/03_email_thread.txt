Sam,

Looping in Billing for the credit application piece. To recap the flow (sorry for the long note):

1) You POST a lead to our intake (XML). We validate the required fields and formats, compute a combined score (contact + academic) using parameters that Corporate and the schools maintain. Those parameters won’t change mid-day; updates happen in maintenance windows with access control.

2) Based on the total score: high -> we assign to a rep (using the latest available list we pull from CRM on a schedule) and immediately send to the Enterprise CRM, including the score. medium -> it lands in eLeads for Enrollment Coordinator review. When Maria clicks verify, we assign a rep then submit to CRM. low -> we return it to you right away with a specific reason, plus the data you sent for reconciliation.

3) If the CRM flags a potential duplicate either synchronously or later, we store that ‘potential duplicate’ marker with the lead on our side. That’s visible internally; for returns to you, if duplicate is the rejection reason, we label it accordingly. Not every potential duplicate causes a rejection, just noting.

4) Regarding timing: during 8 AM–8 PM Central, our overall end-to-end target allows up to about 5 minutes for the majority of traffic. Outside those hours, we’re aiming for almost all leads to complete in around 3 minutes. Within that, the washing step itself should usually finish in under ~25 seconds. If it doesn’t, we log it as a slow event.

5) When you agree a lead is invalid on your side, you submit a credit record to the enterprise billing interface you already have. Finance (cc’d) will make sure it gets applied properly. We don’t have a new API for that.

There’s still an open action on our side to confirm whether CRM’s ARA will honor our pre-assigned rep or re-evaluate; either way, the lead will have a rep before we hand it over.

Let us know if any of that conflicts with your expectations. Also, we’ve had a couple of partners ask for JSON—just to reiterate, we can’t accept that in v1 due to platform constraints.

Thanks,
