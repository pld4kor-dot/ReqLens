Hi Delmiro,

Quick follow-ups after the session last week:

1) Printing: To make screenshots usable for journals, I’m planning a default look that’s essentially dark markers/axes on white, minimal shading. Any objections to prioritizing print clarity over on-screen “prettiness”?

2) Axis labels: There was feedback that after rotating, labels must remain clear and not inverted. I can pin their orientation to the camera and add contrast so they don’t blend into the point cloud. Sound good?

3) Marker sizing: I’ve set very small point markers by default, with mild scaling on zoom. The goal is to preserve the overall pattern and avoid a “blob” look when zoomed out. For presentations we can review sizing later, but accuracy comes first.

4) Performance targets: Is it acceptable to say “typical datasets open in ~30 seconds” (size dependent), and that key interactions (selection, changing orientation) will respond within about 2 seconds? I want to put concrete numbers in so we can test against them.

5) Users: Just confirming the target groups are Identifiers, Investigators, and Demonstrators. I’ll bake their needs into the UX.

Hi Ben,

All fine by me in principle. We’ve burned ourselves before with “pretty” visuals that turned to grey mush on printouts. So yes, make it black-on-white friendly by default. We can live without gradients.

Labels: absolutely must be readable no matter how you rotate. We had a demo where Z looked backwards. Please avoid that embarrassment.

Marker size: tiny by default. We care about seeing the pattern, not beach balls. Presenters do sometimes ask for bolder points in a lecture hall, but don’t mislead—defaults should preserve the distribution.

Performance: I’m okay putting numbers: “cold start in under about half a minute for the usual files,” and “interactions within a couple seconds.” If something is slower, call it out early.

Users: yes, those three. Keep it dead simple for Identifiers; Investigators will poke around; Demonstrators need clean screenshots and smooth rotation.

Hi all,

Adding context: For Identifiers (like Kofi), selecting one point to check something needs to be quick—if it spins for more than 2 seconds people think it’s frozen. Same for lassoing a small set; I demo to students and I can’t have a long lag. Regarding the markers, please keep them small—especially in screenshots. In our last paper, the points looked like smudges when downsampled for print.

Re labels: my biggest pain has been when rotating—the letters either flip or get too small. If the font keeps a minimum on-screen size and the letters face the viewer, it’ll save us a lot of grief.

I’ll send three datasets later today (small ~10k, typical ~40–60k, big ~250k). I agree with D: start time under 30 seconds for the middle one is fine; the huge one will be slower and that’s okay as long as it’s acknowledged.

Sara
