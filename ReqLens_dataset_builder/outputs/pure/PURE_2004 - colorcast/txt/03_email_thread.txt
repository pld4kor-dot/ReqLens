Hi all,

Catching up from the last call. I want to codify a few items, and get pushback if I’m misrepresenting anything:

- We’re treating this as version 1.0, no prior live revs at ABC, and we still have the business constraint to go live by end of Q2 2004 (yes, the plan references that legacy date; it matters to finance’s ROI model).
- The environment assumptions: client computers and servers we use will meet or exceed the requirements we specify (browsers, server capacity). If we write performance numbers without tagging them, those are assumed to be client-perceived metrics. If we mean server throughput, we’ll explicitly say so.
- The feature modules remain single-page units (Search, Visual Picker, Sample Match, Translator, Admin). We link between them but do not mash them together in one mega page.
- High availability: keep the wording to ‘highly accessible to clients and distributors via the web’ and let Ops define clustering/DR in their docs.
- We’re okay that nearest colors are computed in RGB space and that’s ‘good enough’ for v1.
- We depend on the vendor’s/third-party paint data repositories for both the paint info and search engine backend (i.e., not rolling our own DB in-house for this module).
- We need to handle adds & retirements of paints/collections during the migration; if something is discontinued, translator should offer the new mapping or suggest closest alternatives.
- We must expose theme/styling hooks so ABC web designers can skin it to match the main site.
- Error handling: include both a user-facing way to report issues and automatic error reports to ColorKast; keep the experience unobtrusive. Also, please include in the constraints section that the system attempts a full recovery on any error.

Thanks,
